Account snapshot

Pharma Client · Pan-European pharmaceutical group · 5 entities (DE, FR, UK, NL, BE) · Compliance Suite + Data Insights · ARR: £615,000 · Renewal: 12 May 2025 · Health score: 27 (critical, declining from 78 in August 2024)

Relationship status in this meeting

CFO — Economic buyer, negative sentiment, silent since January. Has issued a 20% vendor cost reduction mandate. This meeting is your best opportunity to reframe the commercial conversation before procurement makes its recommendation. He responds to ROI data and peer benchmarks, not product pitches.

Procurement Lead — Running the RFP independently. Has met with Veeva twice. Meeting her in this context is a significant opportunity — she has agreed to hold the decision pending your response, which signals she is not yet committed to switching.

IT Director — Supportive, active. Will provide technical validation but cannot override procurement. Pre-brief him privately before the meeting if possible.

Key talking points

  • Open with the ROI case, not a product pitch. Since June 2022: 38% reduction in audit preparation time, 0 reportable compliance incidents in 5 entities, €2.1M estimated regulatory penalty avoided (based on incident modelling). Give the CFO a number he can use internally.
  • Reframe the cost question. A switch to Veeva carries an estimated 9–14 month implementation delay and €800K–1.2M in migration costs across 5 entities. This is the total cost of switching, not just contract value.
  • Present usage-based commercial options before they demand them. Have 3 scenarios prepared: (a) current terms +3% CPI uplift, (b) usage-based model at 15% reduction with commitment floors, (c) multi-year deal with 18% reduction and locked pricing. Let them choose.
  • Request CEO-to-CFO call. the CFO indicated openness. This is the highest-value action coming out of this meeting — if agreed, escalate to your CEO by Thursday.
  • Address Veeva directly. Do not avoid it. Acknowledge that they conducted a strong presentation. Ask Procurement Lead: "What would make our proposal definitively stronger than their offering for you?" Her answer will tell you the real decision criteria.

Risk items to manage

  • Champion gap: Dr. Chen's departure leaves us without a credibility anchor in the compliance function. Do not reference Dr. Chen in the meeting — it signals instability. Focus on IT Director as the continuity voice.
  • Procurement Lead disengagement: If she checks her phone or defers to the CFO throughout, request a bilateral follow-up call this week. Do not let this meeting be the last substantive conversation before decision day.
  • the CFO walking out early: CFOs do this. Have your 90-second ROI summary ready as an opening, and your commercial scenarios as a one-page leave-behind. The meeting can end at any moment.

Recommended next actions from this meeting

  • Leave a written commercial proposal with the three pricing scenarios before end of meeting
  • Secure a date for CEO-to-the CFO call — ideally before 4 April
  • Request a bilateral with Procurement Lead to understand final RFP scoring criteria
  • Ask IT Director to send you the formal procurement evaluation timeline within 48 hours
